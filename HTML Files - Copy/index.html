Part 2: Diffrences in Spending by Injury Location and Injury Type

Injuries occurring in high-risk areas or involving critical body parts often entail greater medical expenses due to the complexity of treatment required. For instance, an injury to the head or spine is likely to incur higher costs compared to a minor cut or bruise on an arm or leg. Similarly, the type of injury significantly influences spending; a fracture or burn might require extensive medical intervention compared to a strain or sprain.
